An adapted vocabulary guide
This lexicon takes the standard language of sales and marketing and reframes it for people whose primary motivation is their work — not the transaction. Every term has been renamed to describe its actual function, with the original industry term kept in brackets so you can use both languages fluently.
Who it's for
Consultants, coaches, designers, developers, and anyone selling a digital product who needs to understand the mechanics of connecting their work to the people who need it — without adopting the mindset of a salesperson.
How the terms are structured
Each entry has three parts:
ADAPTED NAME — what the term actually describes in plain language
// ORIGINAL TERM — the industry-standard name you'll encounter in the wild
DEFINITION — what it means and why it matters
[+] EXAMPLE — one concrete example from one of four service types
The four service types in examples
① CONSULTING — advisory or strategic engagements
② 1-ON-1 WORK — coaching, mentoring, therapy, tutoring
③ DESIGN / BUILD — client projects, commissioned work
④ DIGITAL PRODUCT — courses, tools, templates, software
Reading order
Chapters are designed to follow the natural arc of getting work to people: first understand who they are (Ch.01), then how to reach them (Ch.02), then what to say (Ch.03), then how to reach agreement (Ch.04), then how to measure what worked (Ch.05). You can also dip in anywhere.
