// MAPPING THE PEOPLE AROUND YOUR WORK
VOL I OF I  ·  ADAPTED ED.
TERMS 09
STATUS LOADED
CH 01/05
Before you can connect your work with anyone, it helps to have a shared language for describing who those people are and where they stand. The terms below are a way of sorting — not of ranking people's worth, but of understanding how much common ground already exists between you and someone else. The closer the fit, the less explaining you have to do.
T E R M S   01 – 05
* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *
Contact
// Lead
Anyone who has crossed paths with your work — a form fill, a reply, a handshake. The raw starting point of any list.
[+] EXAMPLE (digital product)
Someone who signed up for your newsletter after finding a free article through search. Interest shown; fit unknown.
Unknown Contact
// Cold Lead
Someone who hasn't encountered your work yet. You're introducing yourself from scratch — no prior signal, no shared context.
[+] EXAMPLE (consulting)
A company you've identified as a strong fit, whose operations lead has no idea you exist.
Already-Curious Contact
// Warm Lead
Someone who showed genuine interest on their own — visited, downloaded, was pointed your way. There's already a thread to pull.
[+] EXAMPLE (1-on-1 work)
Someone following your work for months who finally replied asking how your process works.
Relationship Contact
// Friendly Informal
Someone warm through relationship rather than marketing — a former collaborator, a friend of a friend. The warmth is personal, not earned through output.
[+] EXAMPLE (design / build)
A past client's co-founder who saw your work up close and now has their own product to build.
Good-Fit Contact
// Qualified Lead
Someone who, looking at what they need and what you do, lines up well. Right context, right problem, right circumstances. The filter that matters most.
[+] EXAMPLE (consulting)
A company with a specific operational challenge you've solved before and a decision-maker actively looking for help.
T E R M S   06 – 09
* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *
Someone You're Exploring With
// Prospect
A good-fit contact now in real conversation. You're figuring out whether there's something real here. Not a commitment yet — but no longer just a name.
[+] EXAMPLE (1-on-1 work)
Someone you had an introductory call with who asked to speak again next week.
Your Ideal Person
// ICP — Ideal Collaborator Profile
A clear picture of the person who gets the most from what you do — their situation, their problem, how they think. Not about exclusion; about knowing who you're actually for.
[+] EXAMPLE (digital product)
"Professionals who are good at what they do but have never had to think about how to position or communicate it."
Where Someone Is With Your Work
// Lifecycle Stage
Where someone sits in their arc with you — just discovered, following along, worked with you once, or now referring others. Knowing the stage helps you respond appropriately.
[+] EXAMPLE (design / build)
A past client referring others is at a completely different stage than a first-time portfolio visitor — and needs a different conversation, if any.
A Group You Learn From Together
// Cohort
People who arrived at the same time or through the same path. Looking at them together reveals patterns — what they need, how they engage. Not for labeling; for learning.
[+] EXAMPLE (consulting)
Comparing year-one clients with year-three clients reveals how your ideal engagement has shifted and what kinds of work you do best.
